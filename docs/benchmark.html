Algorithm Reference

1. Standard FDTD (Yee Scheme)

The foundational staggered-grid leapfrog scheme originally developed by Yee (1966) for electromagnetics and adapted for acoustics. Uses second-order centered finite differences for both spatial and temporal derivatives. Pressure and velocity fields are staggered by half a grid cell in both space and time.

Order: O(2,2) Stencil: 7-point CFL: Sc <= 1/sqrt(3) Memory: 8N

2. Compact Explicit FDTD (Kowalczyk-van Walstijn)

A 27-point stencil scheme that combines face, edge, and corner neighbours with optimized weights to minimize numerical dispersion. The compact formulation allows the Courant number to reach Sc = 1 in 3D, making it isotropic-optimal. Dramatically reduces direction-dependent dispersion compared to the standard scheme.

Order: O(2,2)+ Stencil: 27-point CFL: Sc = 1 (optimal) Memory: 3N

3. Interpolated Wideband (IWB)

An extension of the compact scheme that uses interpolation between standard and diagonal Laplacian operators. The interpolation coefficients are optimized for minimal dispersion across the widest possible bandwidth, achieving near-exact phase velocity up to 0.8 of the Nyquist frequency.

Order: O(2,2)++ Stencil: 13-point CFL: Sc = 1 Memory: 3N

4. Digital Waveguide Mesh (Rectilinear)

A physically-inspired model based on networks of digital waveguides. Each junction scatters incoming travelling waves to its neighbours via the scattering equation. The rectilinear topology uses 6-connected nodes. Equivalent to the standard FDTD under certain conditions, but the waveguide formulation offers advantages for passivity analysis and boundary modeling.

Order: O(2,2) Topology: 6-connected CFL: Sc = 1/sqrt(3) Memory: 12N

5. High-Order FDTD (4th-Order Spatial)

Uses 4th-order centered finite differences for spatial derivatives while retaining 2nd-order temporal accuracy. The wider stencil (using i-2, i-1, i, i+1, i+2) provides significantly reduced numerical dispersion at the cost of a slightly stricter CFL condition and more complex boundary treatment. Ideal for long-range propagation.

Order: O(4,2) Stencil: 13-point CFL: Sc <= 0.49 Memory: 8N
